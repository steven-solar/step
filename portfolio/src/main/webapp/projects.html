I developed style guides and outlines for the team, and wrote modules for Binary Search and Linked List problems. The platform is far from finished, but check out our alpha!

SoKat Grant Grader-inator 3000
(we never decided on a name)
Last Summer, while working at SoKat.co, my two teammates and I developed an algorithm to automate deciding which federal grant applications should and should not be funded. We leveraged natural language processing techniques such as Bag of Words, POS tagging and Latent Semantic Indexing to extract data from the application text. This, combined this with "metadata" about the applications, was fed into our model which outputted predictions with a 95% F1 score.
Check out this post about our presentation to ACF execs!
Feed
For a Hopkins-hosted accelerator I, along with a partner, developed the concept of Feed, a mobile application to reduce food waste by streamlining the food rescue process. We gained admission to the spark Accelerator, and won $1K. We spoke with restaurants, grocery stores andvisited soup kitchens to develop our idea. The Spark Accelerator brought in interesting, accomplished speakers and we were able to learn so much about entrepreneurship and the world of Venture Capital. Although we eventually realized it was infeasible due to regulation and health concerns, it was a great learning experience in developing a venture.
